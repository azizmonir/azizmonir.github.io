Abdul Aziz

Brisbane, Australia. azizabdul.mcs@gmail.com

Hello! I’m Aziz. I'm researching on the intersections of digital media, migration and (im)mobility. I extend these themes in various contexts:diaspora & transnationalism, race & ethnicity, religion, cultural diversity & inclusion. Currently, I am pursuing my PhD at Digital Media Research Centre (DMRC), and School of Communication,Queensland University of Technology,Australia. I hold a master’s degree from University of Salzburg, Austria and Vrije Universiteit Brussel, Belgium under the Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters in Digital Communication Leadership (DCLead). My current doctoral research investigates identity politics, integration of the Rohingya diaspora in the age of digital media applying transnational lens and multi-sited qualitative approach. My research interests span from understanding digital and social media to digital (in)equalities and non-media centric approach to digital media and cultural practices in everyday life.

My recent explores the transnational context around issues of digital media, identity and diaspora with a critical approach to social justice, inclusion and cultural diversity. I’m interested in shaping and contributing to projects that involve multicultural abd marginalised communities.
